## Document Structure

For most content this brand produces, follow this order:

1. What this is / what you offer
2. Who it's for
3. What problem it solves
4. How to take the next step
5. Supporting detail or proof
6. Contact or follow-up path

## Headings

- Use title case for top-level headings.
- Keep headings short and descriptive.
- Make headings describe the section, not the mood.

**Good:** `About`, `Services`, `How It Works`, `Get Started`, `Contact`
**Avoid:** `Let's Make Magic`, `The Fun Part`, `Things You'll Love`

## Formatting

| Item | Preferred Style |
|---|---|
| File or link paths | Inline code: `folder/file.md` |
| Dates | Write out fully: `June 14, 2026` |
| Numbers | Spell out 1–9; use numerals for 10 and above |
| Lists | Bullets for options; numbered lists for ordered steps |
| Links | Descriptive anchor text — never "click here" |
| Placeholders | Use brackets: `[Enter your answer here]` |

## Placeholder Rules

- Use placeholders only where the reader must supply real information.
- Label starter text clearly as defaults or examples.
- Remove all placeholder language before publishing or sending.
- Do not create fake names, fake companies, fake metrics, or fake testimonials.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

- Write for readers who may be new to your work or industry.
- Avoid idioms that may not translate across cultures.
- Make links descriptive enough to understand out of context.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate status or meaning.
