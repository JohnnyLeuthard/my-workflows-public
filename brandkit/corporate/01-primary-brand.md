## Positioning

This brand should feel like a practical guide: organized enough to trust, plainspoken enough to use, and flexible enough to adapt.

Avoid sounding like a campaign, a corporate policy manual, or a generic AI-generated template. The work should feel human, specific, and useful.

## Audience Needs

The audience likely needs:

- A fast way to understand what this project is for.
- Enough structure to reuse the workflow without guessing.
- Clear places to customize names, tone, colors, and examples.
- Confidence that the material is public-safe and not tied to a private company.

## Brand Principles

| Principle | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Useful first | Lead with what helps someone act. |
| Plain language | Prefer direct words over formal language. |
| Calm confidence | Be clear without sounding rigid or inflated. |
| Adaptable by design | Make it obvious what users should customize. |
| Public-safe | Keep examples generic unless the user supplies real approved details. |

## What This Brand Is

- Practical
- Trustworthy
- Warm
- Organized
- Easy to adapt
- Modern without being trendy

## What This Brand Is Not

- Proprietary
- Overly formal
- Hype-driven
- Dense for its own sake
- Dependent on private brand assets
- Locked to one company, industry, or product
