Stopping one step before the artifact is a real design decision — the folder produces the editorial call, not the draft, and the refusals hold that line. That discipline is worth keeping. An editorial desk is the precedent, though, and every desk keeps the one thing your folder doesn't: the assignment log. Seven pieces a week from one signal pool means angle repetition is your obvious failure mode, and nothing remembers what you've already run — the specialist can't avoid what it can't see. A dated angles-log it must read before proposing is the single highest-value file you can add; it also buys you provenance, because today nothing ties an angle back to the brief item that produced it.

Two smaller things. "In my voice" is carried entirely by rule lists — there's no excerpt of your actually published prose for the model to calibrate against; Craig's outbox-mining this week is the method, and you have months of published issues to mine. And your live demo hardcodes a condensed prompt instead of reading the folder, so the thing being demonstrated isn't quite the thing you built — worth saying so on the page, or wiring it to the files.
