# Voice and Tone

Use this file when writing documentation, prompts, UI copy, onboarding text, release notes, or workflow guidance.

## Quick Edit Notes

- Voice is the consistent personality.
- Tone changes based on context.
- Replace these defaults with language that fits your audience.

## Brand Voice

The default voice is clear, calm, useful, and human. It should help readers feel oriented without talking down to them.

| Trait | Use This | Avoid This |
|---|---|---|
| Clear | Say what something does and why it matters. | Vague claims or abstract slogans. |
| Warm | Sound like a helpful collaborator. | Forced enthusiasm or excessive friendliness. |
| Capable | Give confident guidance. | Over-explaining simple choices. |
| Practical | Focus on the next useful action. | Long background before the reader can act. |
| Adaptable | Mark what users can change. | Presenting placeholders as final truth. |

## Tone by Situation

| Situation | Tone | Example Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Setup instructions | Direct and steady | Tell the reader what to open, edit, or run. |
| Explaining a concept | Patient and concise | Define the idea, then show how it is used. |
| Warning about risk | Calm and specific | Name the risk and the safer path. |
| Placeholder guidance | Explicit and helpful | Say what should be replaced and what can stay. |
| Error or limitation | Honest and brief | State what is blocked and what is still possible. |

## Writing Defaults

- Use plain language.
- Prefer short paragraphs.
- Use lists when they make scanning easier.
- Explain acronyms the first time they appear.
- Use active voice most of the time.
- Say "you" when speaking directly to the user.
- Use "we" only when representing the project maintainers.

## Phrases That Fit

- "Use this when..."
- "Replace this with..."
- "Start here..."
- "Keep this short..."
- "Choose the option that matches your project..."
- "If this does not apply, remove it..."

## Phrases to Avoid

- "Best-in-class"
- "World-class"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Seamless experience"
- "Leverage synergies"
- "Unlock value"
- "Game changer"

## Tone Guardrails

- Do not exaggerate capabilities.
- Do not invent proof, metrics, testimonials, or case studies.
- Do not imply official approval from a company or platform unless it exists.
- Do not use fear-based language.
- Do not bury important setup steps in long prose.
