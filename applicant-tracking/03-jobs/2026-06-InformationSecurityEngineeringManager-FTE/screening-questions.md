# Screening Questions — 2026-06-InformationSecurityEngineeringManager-FTE

Use these questions during phone screens or hiring panel reviews. Behavioral questions use the STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

---

## Behavioral Questions

**1. PAM Modernization Leadership**
Tell me about a time you led a team through a significant PAM or identity platform modernization — for example, retiring hard-coded credentials, eliminating shared accounts, or migrating to a policy-driven access model. What was your approach, and what did you learn?

**2. Cross-Functional Influence**
Describe a situation where you had to drive privileged access adoption across multiple teams (cloud, infrastructure, application, IAM) that had competing priorities. How did you build alignment and move the initiative forward?

**3. Talent Development**
Give an example of how you've grown technical depth in a security engineering team. What did you do to identify gaps, develop individuals, and retain high performers?

**4. Incident Leadership**
Walk me through a privileged access-related incident you led. How did you manage the response, communicate to stakeholders, and ensure the root cause was addressed to prevent recurrence?

**5. Competing Priorities**
Tell me about a time you had to balance long-term platform roadmap work against urgent security risk reduction or audit-driven remediation. How did you prioritize and communicate tradeoffs?

---

## Technical Questions

**6. CyberArk Architecture**
Walk me through how you would architect a CyberArk deployment to support 500+ application teams onboarding non-human identities, while keeping engineering overhead low. What self-service or automation patterns would you use?

**7. DevSecOps Integration**
How would you integrate PAM controls into a CI/CD pipeline? Specifically, how do you handle secrets injection for ephemeral workloads without breaking developer velocity?

**8. Zero Trust and Cloud-Native PAM**
How does your approach to privileged access change in a Kubernetes / cloud-native environment compared to a traditional on-prem model? What are the key controls, and where do traditional PAM tools fall short?
